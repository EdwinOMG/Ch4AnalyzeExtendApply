Hexadecimal, RGB, and HSL color codes all represent colors in different ways. Hexadecimal uses a six-digit code, RGB uses a combination of red, green, and blue values, and HSL uses hue, saturation, and lightness values. Each has its advantages and is suitable for different scenarios.

Preferred color code format: hexadecimal. Hexadecimal codes are easy to understand, widely supported, and provide a wide range of color options.

HWB color model is an alternative to HSL and HSV. It represents colors in terms of hue, whiteness, and blackness, providing a more intuitive way to describe color.

CMYK (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Key) color model is primarily used in printing. It describes colors in terms of the four primary ink colors used in printing presses.
